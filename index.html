Restructure Student Assembly Meetings:

I intend to work with both SA members and community members to build a better meeting style and atmosphere for the SA. The current layout of SA meetings does not make anyone feel welcome nor does it allow for productive conversations. This needs to be changed.


Image courtesy of the Cornell Daily Sun

Better Organizational Support:

The Student Assembly must keep better records of the work done by the Appropriations Committee. The committee will spend the next academic year advising and regulating organizations funded directly by the SA. If the committee does extensive and transparent work during the off-cycle year, there will be fewer surprises for the SA, organizations, and the larger community when new allocations are decided in Fall 2019.

Update the Campus Code of Conduct:

There exist few ways in which Student Assembly members can directly and immediately impact life on campus. One of those is through the power granted to the university assemblies to amend the Campus Code of Conduct. Following the events of last semester and ongoing incidences of verbal and physical attacks, multiple groups on campus have been investigating the best way to

incorporate language concerning hate speech and harassment into the Campus Code of Conduct

or into the enforcement practices of the Judicial Administrator. This work will not end this semester, and I will actively work to ensure that undergraduate voices are included in these discussions..

Publicize the Climate Action Plan:

The University has an extensive Climate Action Plan which includes a 2035 Carbon Neutrality goal centered on the experimental dream of a working Earth Source Heat system. Most students and faculty do not know any specifics about the plan. In 2016, the administration almost erased the 2035 date from their plans. If the greater Cornell community is going to hold the University administration to their word, there must be a broader awareness for the Climate Action Plan, and the University itself must play a role in educating the community.
